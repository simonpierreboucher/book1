8  Assumption of OLS Estimators

We will now see in depth that the assumption measure must be present in order to obtain an unbiased estimator.

8.1 Unbiasedness of OLS

\[ \hat{\beta} =(X'X)^{-1}X'Y \\ =(X'X)^{-1}X'(X \beta+\epsilon) \\ =(X'X)^{-1}X'X \beta +(X'X)^{-1}X' \epsilon \\ =\beta+(X'X)^{-1}E(X'\epsilon) \]

Since \(E[\epsilon \mid X]=0\), the OLS estimator is unbiased. \[ E[\hat{\beta}]=\beta \]

8.2 Variance of OLS Estimators

\[ Var(\hat{\beta})=Var[\beta+(X'X)^{-1}X' \epsilon] =Var[(X'X)^{-1}X' \epsilon] \\ =E[(X'X)^{-1}X'\epsilon \epsilon' X(X'X)^{-1}]\\ =(X'X)^{-1}X'E(\epsilon \epsilon') X(X'X)^{-1}\\ =(X'X)^{-1}X'\sigma^2 X(X'X)^{-1} \]

Since \((X'X)^{-1}(X'X)=1\)

\[ Var(\hat{\beta})=\sigma^2(X'X)^{-1} \]

8.3 Assumption of the Classical Linear Regression Model

  1. Linearity: The model specifies a linear relationship between y and x

\[y=X \beta+\epsilon\]

  1. Full rank : There is no exact linear relationship among any of the independent variables in the model.

  2. Exogeneity of the independent variables : This means that the independent variables will not carry useful information for prediction of \(\epsilon\).

\[$E[\epsilon \mid X]=0\]

  1. Homoscedasticity and nonautocorrelation : \(\epsilon\) has the same finite variance.

\[E[\epsilon'\epsilon \mid X]=\sigma^2 I\]

  1. Data generation : X may be fixed or random.

  2. Normal distribution : The disturbances are normally distributed.

\[\epsilon \mid X \sim N(0,\sigma^2 I)\]
